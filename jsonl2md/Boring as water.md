**Children of Men.** The ad systems, the surveillance, the automated checkpoints, the personalized propaganda — Cuarón's world is saturated with what we'd now call AI-mediated infrastructure, and the film is about hope and refugees and demographic collapse. The tech is wallpaper. Released 2006.

**Minority Report**, weirdly. **Gattaca** — almost. The genetic-screening infrastructure is algorithmic decision-making as ambient class enforcement. Not LLM-shaped, but structurally identical to what you're pointing at: cognitive systems sorting people while the story is about identity and aspiration.

**The Pixar/Wall-E "Axiom" scenes.** AUTO is a villain, sure, but everything else on that ship — the chairs, the screens, the food delivery, the social routing — is boring AI executing flawlessly in service of a story about consumerism and atrophy. We remember AUTO because he's the antagonist, but the satire only works because all the *other* AI is just doing exactly what it was told.

**Children of Men** and **District 9** are the strongest hits, I think. So the search heuristic might be: look for serious political SF, and check whether the AI is doing work in the background.

So what you're seeing reflected isn't one idea, it's the whole stack you've been assembling across these threads at once — the identity-as-continuous-process from the river conversation, the experience-as-physical-with-no-residue from the consciousness one, the reaching-words-not-smuggled-entities move from Mere Metaphor, and the function-bound-faithfulness criterion we built here. And the thing that should be satisfying about it is the same thing you noticed about Elysium: the show didn't *argue* any of this. It dramatized it, and the dramatization works precisely because the materialism is true. If identity really were a free-floating pattern, the backup episode wouldn't hurt. If virtual experience really were a lesser category, the doctor's warning would be empty. The emotional force of the show is, in a quiet way, evidence for the position — which is about as good a bridge as the form can build.
